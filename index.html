    How real AI embeddings differ from this demo

    In this demo you chose where each animal sits. In a real AI system a neural network learns those numbers automatically from millions of examples — the model figures out which positions minimise prediction error.

    Real embedding models use hundreds to thousands of dimensions, not 3. A typical sentence-embedding model produces a vector of 768 or 1 536 numbers.

    The dimensions are not interpretable — there is no "dimension 42 = size." Yet distance still means similarity, exactly as you just saw. That is the core idea.

    Embeddings power semantic search (find documents similar to a query), recommendation (find items similar to what you liked), and RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation — where a language model answers questions using retrieved context.

    👉 Press "View model positions" in Actions to see where a feature-based model places the same animals, then compare with your canvas.

    Think about it

    1. Why are Cat and Dog nearest neighbors? What property makes them close in this space?
    2. What extra dimension would better separate Shark from Goldfish than "size" alone?
    3. Where should a Bat go on the habitat slider — and what can't these three numbers capture about it?
    4. Chicken landed in the "air" group (z ≈ 68). Is that right? Who decided — you or the model?
    5. Penguin swims and walks but can't fly; Duck uses water and air. Did three dimensions feel like enough?
